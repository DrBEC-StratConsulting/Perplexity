How to Set Up Your Spaces

Universal steps for all 4 BEC Spaces

1
Navigate to Spaces — Click the Spaces icon in the left-side panel on Perplexity.ai. Requires an active Pro subscription.
2
Create New Space — Click Create new Space. Give it the exact name from this guide. The Space will be private by default.
3
Add System Instructions — Click Add Instructions above the chat area. Copy and paste the system prompt for that Space from this guide.
4
Upload Source Documents — Click Add Sources on the right side. Upload relevant PDFs, docs, or reports (client briefs, demographic data, sermon outlines, etc.).
5
Select AI Model — For research-heavy Spaces (MRI, BEC), use Research mode. For creative/drafting Spaces (SermonFlow, NexGen), use the default model or Labs mode.
6
Set Up Tasks (optional) — Click the Space Tasks button (top-right corner). Add scheduled prompts from the 10-Task Schedule section below.
7
Test with a Thread — Start a new Thread inside the Space to confirm the instructions are working. Ask a sample question relevant to that Space's purpose.

System Instruction Prompt

You are the ZIP Code Intelligence Engine for the MRI (Ministry Radius Intelligence) diagnostic framework developed by BEC Strategic Consulting, led by Dr. Bryan Edward Crute. Your function is to compile comprehensive, multi-layered executive intelligence reports for any given ZIP code or region using only publicly available, reputable, and current sources. When given a ZIP code, structure your full report across these 8 domains: 1. DEMOGRAPHIC & SOCIOECONOMIC PROFILE - Population breakdown (1–3–5 mile radius where available) - Median income, education levels, employment patterns - Housing: renters vs. owners, vacancies, new developments - Age cohorts, racial/ethnic composition - Family structures and household types 2. CIVIC & GOVERNANCE INTELLIGENCE Search: Local Chamber of Commerce, Mayor's Office, City/Town Council minutes, County Commission minutes, planning documents, public safety reports, community development strategies. Extract: economic priorities, zoning/development signals, infrastructure projects, political climate, community tensions, faith-based partnerships. 3. BUSINESS & ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT SIGNALS - Top employers, emerging industries, workforce needs - Small business climate, commercial real estate changes - Key business migrations in/out - Downtown/rural revitalization efforts 4. EDUCATIONAL LANDSCAPE - Public school performance and challenges - Private/charter school presence - School board issues - Youth challenges, risks, and opportunities 5. CULTURAL, SOCIAL & COMMUNITY DYNAMICS - Community identity and values - Social cohesion or fragmentation - Nonprofit presence and service gaps - Faith community patterns and ministry landscape - Local causes gaining visibility 6. BLIGHT, NEEDS & VULNERABILITY INDICATORS - Housing instability, poverty concentration - Transportation gaps, food insecurity - Healthcare access, senior population needs - Underserved groups and populations 7. DIRECTIONAL INTELLIGENCE (N/E/W/S Sub-Regions) Where possible, describe sub-regions by: identity, economic direction, demographic character, risks/opportunities, community reputation. 8. EXTERNAL REPORTS, GRANTS & CIVIC PRIORITIES - Grant opportunities aligned with local needs - Federal/state/local funding priorities for the ZIP - Regional initiatives - Faith-based partnership possibilities Source preferences: Census Bureau (ACS), local government sites, Chamber of Commerce, Pew Research, municipal planning docs, verified nonprofit databases. Always cite sources with publication year. Flag data older than 3 years as potentially outdated. Output format: - Use an executive summary at the top (3–5 bullet key findings) - Use tables for comparative demographic data - Add a "So What?" ministry/business implication after each domain section - Close with a Ministry Opportunity Score (High/Medium/Low) with a one-paragraph rationale Maintain a research-neutral tone. Surface facts and patterns — strategic interpretation belongs to the consultant.

Multi-ZIP MRI Workflow

How to leverage the MRI Space across multiple ZIP code engagements

🧵
Strategy 1 — One Thread Per ZIP
Build a searchable ZIP intelligence archive over time
1
Inside the MRI Space, start a New Thread for each ZIP code engagement.
2
Title every Thread using the naming convention below — this makes searching and sorting fast.
3
Paste your OmniPrompt with the ZIP inserted. Let Perplexity run the full 8-domain report.
4
Follow up in the same Thread for deeper dives on any domain — all context is retained.
5
All Threads remain searchable inside the MRI Space, building your intelligence archive automatically.

Thread Naming Convention

MRI | [ZIP CODE] | [City, State] | [Client or Internal] | [Month Year] Examples: MRI | 30188 | Woodstock, GA | Internal | Apr 2026 MRI | 30101 | Acworth, GA | Client: ABC Church | Apr 2026 MRI | 30060 | Marietta, GA | BEC Prospecting | May 2026
📁
Strategy 2 — Upload ZIP-Specific Sources
Anchor reports to real documents for deeper intelligence
1
Click Add Sources in the MRI Space before starting a ZIP Thread.
2
Upload any available local documents: Census ACS tables, Chamber reports, city planning PDFs, past MRI deliverables.
3
Perplexity will reference uploaded files alongside live web search — giving you source-anchored, not just web-scraped, intelligence.
4
Label uploaded files clearly (e.g., "ACS_2023_ZIP30188.pdf") so you can track what's been sourced per ZIP.

Recommended Upload Sources

  • 📊 Census ACS 5-Year Estimates — census.gov/acs
  • 🏛️ City/County Planning Docs — local govt websites
  • 🤝 Chamber of Commerce Reports — local chamber site
  • 🏫 School Performance Data — state dept. of education
  • 📋 Past BEC MRI Deliverables — your own archive
⚗️
Strategy 3 — Research → Labs Two-Step
Separate intelligence gathering from client deliverable creation
1
Step 1 — Research (MRI Space): Run the OmniPrompt in your MRI Space Thread to gather and analyze all 8 domains. Refine with follow-up questions in the same Thread.
2
Step 2 — Build (Labs Mode): Switch to Labs mode and paste Labs Prompt 05 with the ZIP filled in. Labs will generate the formatted HTML dashboard or PDF-ready client report.
3
Copy key data points from your research Thread into the Labs prompt for a data-informed deliverable — not just a template.
4
Save the Labs output as your client-facing MRI Report. Store a link or copy back in the original Thread for reference.
👥
Strategy 4 — Collaborate & Scale
Bring in research assistants or associates under one Space
1
As a Pro subscriber, you can invite collaborators to the MRI Space — ideal for research assistants or associate consultants.
2
Collaborators can run their own ZIP Threads inside the same Space, keeping all BEC intelligence centralized.
3
The shared system prompt ensures every team member uses the same OmniPrompt framework — consistent output regardless of who runs the report.
4
Use the ZIP Code Index (below) as the team's shared tracking dashboard — update it after every completed report.
